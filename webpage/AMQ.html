      What video hosts does the game support?

      The game supports the open host Catbox and the closed hosts Animethemes and Openings.moe.
      The open hosts allows anyone to upload and share files on them, while the closed hosts doesn't allow users to upload, but already have a large library of videos.

      What version of a song should I use?

      You should always use the version that was used in the show. For openings and endings thatâ€™s usually the 1.30 minute cut version. In the case that different versions are used throughout the show, you should go with the one most commonly used, if theyâ€™re used equally for with the first one.

      Can I use a still image instead of the video?

      For openings/endings you should always use the actual video.
      For insert songs video versions (with potential background noise/talk edited out) is strongly preferred, but still image versions might get approved until an edited version becomes available.

      What should be included in the video?

      The opening/ending/scene the song plays in.
      If a song plays with talking or other sounds during the scene, replace the audio with an identical clean version.

      What file formats are accepted?

      Video files should be in webm format, and sound files in mp3. Any other formats will be rejected by the scan bot.

      What are the quality requirements?

      The song must not be pitched, sped up, slowed down or remixed in any way from their original versions. Any silence at the start or end of the song should be cut out. Likewise, extended notes at the end of the song should be cut short.
      The video should also be of decent quality and not contain hardsubs, through exceptions can be made for older or rare shows where a better quality isnâ€™t available.

      What are keyframes?

      Keyframes are special frames set when the video is encoded. They allow for skipping in the video without having to load the rest of the video up to the point skipped to.
      For AMQ a minimum of 1 keyframe for each 10 seconds of video is required, anything less will get rejected by the bot. Itâ€™s recommended to have 1 keyframe per 5 second.

      What happens once my file has been submitted?

      After your song have been submitted, itâ€™ll enter the queue to be scanned by the scan bot. The scan bots checks if the encoding, resolution and keyframe count of the file are correct. If the file is already approved on another host, the bot will automatically approve the upload or else itâ€™ll pass it to the moderator team, who will validate that the song is correct and check the quality of the video.
      If your song is rejected, youâ€™ll receive a server message about it and the Notes + xp rewarded for the upload are removed.

      What songs are shown in the Expand Library?

      The Expand Library shows all songs currently in the database for anime that are in your linked lists, so long as they do not have mp3, 480, 720p qualities up on catbox, along with a link to the uploads on animethemes and openings.moe (if available). Additionally if a song is in the approval queue and has no other qualities uploaded, it will not appear in the expand library either.

      A song is missing from the database, how do I get it added?

      First make sure the song isnâ€™t already in the database: Doesnâ€™t appear in the Expand Library or in game (do a solo quiz with only that songs show in your list).
      If it wasnâ€™t in the Expand Library or in game, you can request to have it added on the games Discord. Make sure to read the FAQ channel and pins in the #database-fixes channel, for how to request it.

      Last Man Standing Rules

      In Last Man Standing each players start with 1-5 lives (depending on game settings).

      To win, you have to be the last player remaining alive

      If a living player answers correctly, all other living players who didn't guess correctly losses one life

      When you die, the game isnâ€™t over. For each song you answer correctly after dying youâ€™ll earn a respawn point, and if you manage to collect 3 respawn points, you'll respawn back into the fight! But if you get a song wrong, youâ€™ll lose all your respawn points.
      Finally, to get the third and last respawn point, you must not only guess the song correctly, but all living players must get it wrong as well.

      Battle Royale Rules

      In Battle Royale, the game has an added loot phase

      In the loot phase, players first chose a drop point on the map, and then move around collecting anime. Ones the loot phase is over, a Last Man Standing quiz is started, with only songs from the anime collected in the loot phase

      In the loot phase, you chose a drop point by clicking a tile on the map. After dropping you can move around with WASD or arrow keys, and collect anime scattered around the map by clicking on them ones you get close to them

      Data Stores are also scattered around the map, and contain several anime, but can first be opened ones you get close to them

      You can change between map tiles by standing on the gates at the edge of the tiles and clicking on them

                        What is the Avatar Drive?

                        The Avatar Drive is your way to support the game, while at the same time influencing what the future avatars will be. The system is pretty straightforward, when donating you can chose to donate towards any avatar in the Avatar Drive or nominate a new avatar. The value donated is then added to that avatars score.
                        When the time comes to add a new avatar to the game, whoever is leading the Avatar Drive is chosen.

                        When Are New Avatars Added to the Game?

                        A new avatar is added to the game once the drive has raised enough money to cover the production cost of adding the avatar and running the game. This value can vary depending on the length of the drive. Calculating the length of the drive and the amount to be raised will be worked out after the first couple of drives. Based on the experience we will add a clear goal to be reached for a new avatar to be added! The goal is to have a new avatar enter the game every 1-2 months.
